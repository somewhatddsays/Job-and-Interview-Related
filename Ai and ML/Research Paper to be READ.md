# How 15 Research Papers Created the AI Revolution You're Living In

## The foundational papers behind `ChatGPT`, `Claude`, `Gemini` and every `modern AI`. Read any one — understand the whole field.

### The 15 Papers

| #  | Paper                                | Year / Org              | Why it mattered                                                            | Link                                                         |
|:---|:-------------------------------------|:------------------------|:---------------------------------------------------------------------------|:-------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1  | Attention Is All You Need            | 2017 — Google           | Invented the Transformer. Every modern model runs on this.                 | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762)                    |
| 2  | BERT                                 | 2018 — Google           | Bidirectional understanding. Powered Google Search's biggest leap.         | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805)                    |
| 3  | GPT-2                                | 2019 — OpenAI           | Scaling = emergent abilities. OpenAI called it "too dangerous to release." | [openai](https://openai.com/research/gpt-2-1-5b-release)     |
| 4  | Scaling Laws for Neural LMs          | 2020 — OpenAI           | Proved bigger models + more data = predictably better.                     | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361)                    |
| 5  | GPT-3                                | 2020 — OpenAI           | 175B parameters. In-context learning. The "holy shit" moment.              | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165)                    |
| 6  | Vision Transformer (ViT)             | 2020 — Google           | Transformers work on images too, not just text.                            | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.11929)                    |
| 7  | DALL-E                               | 2021 — OpenAI           | Text-to-image. Birth of generative art.                                    | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.12092)                    |
| 8  | Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) | 2020 — Meta             | LLMs + external knowledge = grounded, factual answers.                     | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.11401)                    |
| 9  | LoRA                                 | 2021 — Microsoft        | Fine-tune billion-param models on a single laptop.                         | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.09685)                    |
| 10 | InstructGPT / RLHF                   | 2022 — OpenAI           | Human feedback alignment. Made ChatGPT possible.                           | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155)                    |
| 11 | Chain-of-Thought Prompting           | 2022 — Google           | "Let's think step by step" unlocked reasoning in LLMs.                     | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903)                    |
| 12 | Constitutional AI                    | 2022 — Anthropic        | AI that self-corrects against principles. Powers Claude.                   | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073)                    |
| 13 | AlphaFold 2                          | 2021 — DeepMind         | Solved protein folding. Won the Nobel Prize.                               | [nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-03819-2) |
| 14 | Denoising Diffusion (DDPM)           | 2020 — UC Berkeley      | The math behind Stable Diffusion, Midjourney, DALL-E 2.                    | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239)                    |
| 15 | ReAct                                | 2022 — Google/Princeton | LLMs that reason AND use tools. Foundation of AI agents.                   | [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03629)                    |
